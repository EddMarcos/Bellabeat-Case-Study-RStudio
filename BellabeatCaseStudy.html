Bellabeat is a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart products that inform and inspires women worldwide. Collecting data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health has allowed Bellabeat to empower women with knowledge about their health and habits. I have been asked to focus on one of Bellabeat’s products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers use their smart devices. She believes that analyzing Bellabeat’s available consumer data would reveal more growth opportunities and has asked for some recommendations. The insights I discover will help guide the marketing strategy for the company.

Bellabeat has several products, but for this analysis, we will focus on the application. The Bellabeat app provides users with health data related to their activity, sleep, stress, menstrual cycle, and mindfulness habits. This data can help users understand their current practices and make healthy decisions.

Ask Bellabeat will like to understand how other smart device users use their devices to monitor and improve their health and wellness to help them unlock new growth opportunities for the company. The key stakeholders include:

Urška Sršen — Chief Creative Officer and Bellabeat’s Co-founder. Sando Mur — Mathematician and Bellabeat’s Co-founder. Bellabeat’s marketing analytics team, a team of data analysts.

Plotting a few explorations

I wanted to know the relationship between steps taken in a day and sedentary minutes?

ggplot(data=dailyActivity_merged, aes(x=TotalSteps, y=SedentaryMinutes)) + geom_point()

The graph shows us that the more sedentary minutes the less steps are taken, meaning that the more people are layimg down or sitting, practicing a sedentary behaviour the less are the total steps taken which makes total sense. meaning there is correlation between sedentary minutes and total Steps pattern. Thus can mean that participants are mostly practicing sedentary behavioue which do not able to stay active, this can be bad for their health which can make them lazier. This can alert the user to start walking more by measure the steps they have already taken.

I wanted to know the relationship between minutes asleep and time in bed?

ggplot(data=sleep_day, aes(x=TotalMinutesAsleep, y=TotalTimeInBed)) + geom_point()

What can we can observe in the graph above is that the more time poeple spend in bed, the more they tend to spend more time sleeping.

What could these trends tell you about how to help market this product? Or areas where you might want to explore further? This trend can express the fact that user spend more time in bed, which causes them to sleep more as it shows in the graph above. the app could make an effort to track bed-time and minues asleep patterns and warn users so that they can be alert knwoing that the more they spend time in bed the more they tend to sleep

Act Here are my recommendations. The marketing team should focus on making users comphrehend the importance of contantly exercising for the benefit of their health and general well-being.

Highlighting how the watch can help them track their progress by using it to set activity goals per day and reminders to stay active. By marketing its “get up and move” reminder feature which can help users complete their activity each day.

The marketing team should target not just women who do not own a tracker but women who do as well, and adding a period tracking feature will prove useful.
